Why Valencia?

A city where history and modernity dance in perfect harmony

This Spanish city beckons with its sun-kissed beaches, futuristic architecture, and vibrant cultural tapestry. Founded by the Romans in 138 BC, Valencia's name originates from the Latin term "Valentia," meaning "strength" or "valor."

Imagine strolling through the winding streets of its charming old town, discovering hidden gems around every corner, from bustling markets brimming with local delicacies to the serene beauty of the Turia Gardens. Valencia's avant-garde City of Arts and Sciences, juxtaposed against its centuries-old cathedrals, offers a feast for the eyes and soul.

Visit Valencia, and let this enchanting city captivate your senses and inspire your spirit.

HOT SPOTS

My favourite spots in Valencia

Mercat Central de València

From fresh produce to sweet treats, the Central Market in Valencia is a stand out for its rich and varied cuisine. With over 1200 stalls and considered to be the largest fresh produce market in Europe, this market is a must-see wheb visiting Valencia. Your taste-buds would definitely agree!

Address:

Pl. Ciutat de Bruges, s/n, Ciutat Vella, 46001 València

Learn more

Old Town

Be transported back in time as you visit Valencia's Old Town. Here, you get to experience the history and culture as you walk through the old streets, passing some of the major attraction such as Plaza de la Reina, the Silk Exchange (La Lonja de la Seda), Plaza de la Virgin, and Portal de la Valldigna and the magnificent Church of San Nicolas.

Address:

Pl. de Sant Jaume, 1, Ciutat Vella, 46003 València

Learn more

Sibarizza

Although it's about 20 minutes from the city, this café in Alboraya is definitely worth the visit and is less than 1 minute from the beach. With a medeterranean inspired menu, delicious coffee and cocktails, you can treat yourself to some scrumptous food while admiriing the beauty of the sea. You'll also find a surf shop and school next door should you decide to take up some lessons.

Address:

Plaza Comunión de San Juan, 2, Ciutat Vella
